A Monk Who Rule The World 'Swami Vivekananda'

Who is Swami Vivekananda

SWAMI VIVEKANANDA was one of the most famous and influential leaders of the Vedanta philosophy. He was the chief disciple of Rmakrishna Paramhansa and was the founder of the Ramakrishna Math and Ramkrishna Mission. Many consider him an Icon for his fearless courage, his possitive exhortation to the youth, his broad outlook on social problems and countless lectures and discourses on vedanta philosophy.

Early Life of Swami Vivekananda as Narendra Datta

Swami Vivekananda's real name was NARENDRA DATTA.Narendra Dtta was born into a prominent calcutta (now known an kolkata) family on January 12 , 1863. His father was a well known lawyer and his mother was cultured and aristocratic woman. As a boy ,Narendra was an excellent athelte and superlative student. he read everything ravenously and retaind everything he read. Never content to believe what he was told , he always demanded incontrovertible proof or experiential cerainty before he would believe anything.

Vivekananda's ZEAL towards god

Vivekananda was deeply religious and this same interigty coloured his quest for god.He went one religious man to another and asked them if they had seen God. Finally, met Sri Ramakrishna who was the only one to tell yes, he had seen God and could show Narendra too. Thus began the discipleship of this highly educated and cultured boy to the mostly illiterate rustic temple priest saint. After five years of training , his teacher died. Before Sri Ramakrishna had named Narendra, the leader of all his disciples, young Narendra took the Monastic name , Swami Vivekananda and founded a monastery where he and his brother disciples could carry on their spipritual practices . Following Indian monastic tradition.

Life of Vivekananda as a MONK and SPIRITUAL LEADER

swamiji spent many years travelling through all parts of indian subcontinent as a monk and teach people the real way of life . In May 1893, a group of his disciples sent the swamiji to the United states to attend the parliament of world religious that was being conducted in conjuction with world's fair Chicage. His speeches at the parliament so impressed the listeners that Swamiji was invited to speak all araound Chicago , and then all through the eastern United states. Everywhwere he went, he inspired people with his master's message : EACH SOUL IS DIVINE AND THE GOAL OF LIFE IS FOR EACH OF US TO REALISE THAT DIVINITY FOR OURSELVES

Book Written By Swamiji

Swamiji wrote books on four YOGAS
  1. Raja yoga
  2. Karma Yoga
  3. Bhakti Yoga
  4. Jnana Yoga

The book written by him are very influential and still seen as fundamental texts for any intersted in the Hindu practice of yoga. His letters are of great literary and spiritual value. He was also very good singer and a poet. He had composed many songs including godess, kali the Mother. He used humour for his teachings.His language is very free flowing. His own bengali writings stand stand testimony to the fact that he believed that words- spoken or written should be for making things easier to understand rather than shoe off the speaker or writer's knowledge.

death of Swami Vivekananda

IN fitting irony , swamiji died or went in ultimate meditation (SAMADHI)on July 4, 1902 at the age of 39.Swamiji preached strenght, freedom, manliness and that every soul is divine , he touched the hearts and minds, and changed the lives of many thousands of men and women around the world.
